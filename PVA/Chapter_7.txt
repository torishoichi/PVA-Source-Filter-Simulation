and resonance:harmonic circumstances than non-treble voices. Where

Whoop Timbre and Beyond
Once a treble voice has tracked 1 fo with the /f_irst resonance as high as 
possible through vowel opening (to ca. Bb5), they will need to raise their 
larynx to some degree for higher pitches to continue tracking the sung 
pitch. When the /f_irst resonance can be raised no further, the acoustic 
register makes a transition into whistle register ( mechanism three ) in 
which the /f_irst and second resonances might possibly be brought close

resonated between them by their clustered bandwidths, or in which the 
second resonance takes over. Ian Howell’s research on spectral tone color 
provides the most apt explanation to date of the distinct vowel qualities 
that can be heard pitch by pitch in these stratospheric resonance tunings. 
!ey are typically perceived as bright variants of mid to open vowels [ ɔ

G6. In some cases, an extremely high pitch may be being resonated by the
